THIS IS AN OMAKE

(For non-Japanese speakers: an omake is a 'what if?' story, it's not part of canon. In other words, this didn't really happen in 2YE.)

Parts of the following may end up being lifted into canon, but for now it's just a bonus. I had so much fun with the idea I had to share it, but I can't actually put it in canon or I have to give it to the villains too, at which point the story is just over and the heroes die instantly.

"Ok," I said, carefully choosing my next question for Loki. "You and I have two deals," I began, speaking slowly and carefully. "These deals allow us to ask questions and receive answers from you, with certain provisos. Aside from using these questions, what should we do in order to maximize our information gathering abilities, where the referenced actions can be accomplished within twenty four hours using resources that are actually available to us?"

"Huhhhnnn," the zebra neighed, before morphing into a parrot in a birdcage. "Rawk! Interesting question! Rawk!"

The birdcage morphed again, becoming a gold- and gem-encrusted throne piled high with pillows. A moment later, the bird stretched and deformed itself into Loki, clad in a purple silk suit and spats. He pondered briefly, eyeing his nails as he did.

"Hmmm," he said, puffing on his cigarette holder. "I think I'm not going to answer that. Too many of the other gods would be pissed off if I did."

Well now, that was interesting. Clearly, there was a method that we could actually use to gather large amounts of intelligence relatively cheaply. Cheaply enough that the gods would be pissed about mortals knowing it. Hm. That could actually be a problem; if we figured it out, Ilara or one of her buddies might show up again to twist my head off. Still, it would be incredibly useful....

"I'd like to use an 'truthful but maybe deceptive and you can't refuse to answer' question, please," I asked. "Let's assume that there is an action X that you were thinking of when you decided not to answer my question about how we could maximize our information gathering abilities. Let's assume that there is a set Y of gods who would prevent us from using X and/or would interfere with our use of it. What actions can we take to reduce set Y to the null set?"

Loki pondered that for a minute, his lips moving silently as he repeated the question to himself silently several times. Eventually, a slow smile spread over his face. "Another interesting question. The vast majority of gods get upset when mortals start acquiring too much information; it makes it too easy for them to ascend. On the other hand, we like it when our worshippers find ways to acquire lots of information, because it tends to increase our power in the world and, if they do ascend, they end up being minor deities who owe us a lot of favors—great material for lieutenants, in other words. And, in the very rare case that we see someone doing that who isn't already dedicated to one of us, then everyone gets excited about recruiting him.

"Given all that, there's a push-pull about the whole thing. There's no specific treaty about it but we don't generally bother anyone who has found a major information source unless they start abusing it...pulling information out of it too quickly, or pulling out information that is clearly too valuable to allow a mortal to have.

"Anyway, if there were such a thing as your Action X, then you'd just need to not be a jerk about it." Suddenly I was facing an Ancient Master, straight out of Kung Fu Theatre: a little old Asian man in a white gi with an age-tattered, faded-to-gray belt around his waist. His hands were tucked into his sleeves, a long Fu Manchu mustache drooped down past his chin, and he had an insufferable air of 'I know more than you.'

"In short, grasshopper," the little old stereotype told me. "All you must do is seek the path of wisdom. Do not listen to the 'I', listen only to the Unity. Be at one with the world, do not do battle with it. One who is in battle cannot stay balanced; his feet shall always be driven from the path. Seek the path and remain on it, lest you upset the gods; upset not the gods, for you are crunchy and good with ketchup."

On that somewhat anachronistically jarring image, Loki reverted to his Harlequin form and lounged sideways on his throne, swinging his legs and sipping a Mai Tai.

I leaned my chair back on its rear legs and got all abstracted as I pondered what Loki had said. With all the wacky hijinks and fortune-cookie mumbo jumbo stripped aside, what was the basic message? Apparently, if I could figure out what this divination method was, I could use it as long as I didn't abuse it? Ok, that seemed reasonable.

Loki was staring at me very intently over his drink, and suddenly I had the sense I was missing something. I paused and went back over the words again; Loki had been feeding me information on the sly for a while now...was there more there? I wasn't sure why he didn't just come out and say it, but maybe that just wasn't his style.

Or...huh. There were two reasons to be all coy like this; you wanted to mess with the person you were passing the information to, or you didn't want someone else to know that you were passing the information.

The probability that Loki was messing with me was roughly 1.0...but the probability that he was hiding his information-passing from someone else was not 0. Clearly, the gods enforced rules amongst themselves. Also, Drifa had mentioned something about 'Overgods' which, best guess, were gods who set rules for the gods. Perhaps Loki was hiding from them?

Suddenly the Brainopedia flopped open in front of me and I knew what he was telling me. My chair slapped forward and I stood up so fast that I bumped the table hard enough to spill people's drinks.

"Ok, Loki, got it. Don't be a jerk, don't abuse it. Got it, I'll keep that in mind," I told him hurriedly. "I need to go...uh...use the bathroom. And eat something, and do...a thing. Anyway, thanks so much for coming. Can we use the rest of the questions later?"

He bowed from the waist without getting up, then instantly vanished. For just a moment, I thought I heard very quiet snickering.

I leaned forward, looking around the table with fever-bright eyes. "Come on guys, we really should get out of here in case Afi is watching. We totally need to go seek us some wisdom. Unn, Drifa, I need you to find me a mage and a cleric with some particular spells prepared...."

I led the charge out of the room so fast the Landguard had to work to catch up. My thoughts were racing ahead to how I could avoid getting a divine smackdown for what I was pretty sure was going to be my fourth...fifth?...was going to be yet another worldbreak. Idly, I wondered what it said about me and Stupidworld that I had actually had to create the word 'worldbreak.'

o-o-o-o

I finished writing the last of the many, many pieces of paper, set it on the floor of the tiny cubicle, and closed the door.

Everyone was looking at me in bemusement; I had refused to explain anything until this point. I was giggling about it too much, and I wanted to see the expressions on their faces when I showed them. The first step had been finding someone to cast a Mage's Magnificent Mansion with a specific floorplan. Specifically, it was a single enormous room, the walls of which were entirely covered with high school lockers. The second step had been to get several reams of paper, write one sentence on each, and place each one in a separate locker.

"Ok, here we go," I said, bouncing on my toes in excitement. "Priest Ake, here's what I need you to do." I handed him a paper with written directions.

The blond priest read the directions, looked at me in confusion, then shrugged and did as he was told. "Find the Path to: The locker in this room that contains a paper with a true statement on it!" he declared. Instantly, he walked to the twenty-seventh locker on the left, opened it, and brought me the paper.

"Huh," I said, reading it. "Ok, looks like Afi is at grid square JJ-47 on map 691, less than one mile underground. Unn, I need you to go cast a Polymorph Any Object for me...."

Explanation for the non-D&D players: Find the Path is a cleric spell that gives you instant knowledge of how to get from where you are to anywhere you want to be. It even gives information about "when you get to the red door, the password is 'Swordfish'" and "don't step on the fourth tile or it will explode and shower kittens everywhere." The exploit shown above would never be allowed by any sane DM, but Stupidworld is clearly not run by a sane DM.

For the record, 'Find the Path' does not exist in 2YE.

Also, should you feel the urge to nerdrage about how this wouldn't work because blah blah blah...yes, you're very smart. Hush.
