I found myself sprawled on the rocky floor of the Grǫfhamr canyon with an armful of warm girl on top of me. I'm not sure if Suze was wearing perfume or not, but she smelled fantastic.

~Down boy!~ I told myself firmly. ~Works for you. Also, just baaarely not jailbait. And half your age. Perv.~

"Cough, cough," Loki said, his voice shot through with wild insinuations. (Also, who actually says 'cough, cough'?!) "Should I make a room for you two?"

Suze blushed crimson and hastily scrambled off of me. I was a bit slower; I took a moment to think about glaciers, icy showers, deserts, and everything I could think of related to golf. Once I felt confident in my ability to not embarrass myself, I pushed myself upri—I climbed to my feet.

"Ok, why are we not dead?" I demanded of the world in general. "And why isn't there a giant evil death god of deathy deathness descending on us with fire and sword and death right now?" I turned to the author of my aliveness. "Suze, what did you...how did you...." I stopped. Took a breath. "Suze, could you please explain what happened after we all went through the Teleportation Circle?"

Her shoulders hunched a little bit, and there was just a bit of hesitance in her voice, but she spoke right up and met my eyes. "The Ice Assassins Mindscraped us, M'Lord, and then they took everything we had, so—"

I waved her to silence. "Yes, I was there for that part. Right now I'm actually a bit stuck between 'how did you come up with that plan', 'how did you keep it secret from the Mindscrape', 'how are you suddenly casting ninth level spells'—actually no, you obviously went through the FLEA. But when? You've been beside me this whole time."

She shook her head in firm negation. "No, M'Lord. When I asked permission to go get the magic items, I got them...and I also went through the POPEs and the FLEA. I had a little trouble with the FLEA—none of the people operating it knew who I was, so at first they didn't want to let me cut in the line. When I told them I was your assistant and showed that paper, one of the senior magi thought he remembered seeing me with you at one point, so they let me through."

I started to ask 'but why did you want to?' and realized what a stupid question that was. I had wanted great cosmic power with which to make reality my chewtoy—why shouldn't Suze want that? There was a better question.

"Why didn't you tell us, Suze?" I asked in confusion. "Why keep it secret?"

She looked at me and just for a moment there was anger behind her eyes. "Why did I have to do it myself, M'Lord? Why didn't you send me through when you tried to go?" She raised her chin; the anger was there, and also a lifetime of carefully hidden resentment. "I'm a servant, M'Lord. No one thinks about the servants when there's great things afoot...just when they need a meal, or a pen, or a bedwarmer." Her gaze lost focus as she looked into her own memories; a tiny, hard little smile twisted across her mouth. "They'll remember me now, though," she murmured, so quietly I almost didn't hear.

She shook herself, snapping back to the present. "This was my chance to be safe, M'Lord. Wizard, sorceror, cleric, rogue...I can out-fight any of the nobles in the land with a sword, or I can elude their best hunters, or I can just turn them into bugs and step on them."

I nodded, shamefaced. "I'm sorry. You're absolutely right, I should have sent you through. It was thoughtless, and I apologize."

She looked at me with incredulity. "Um...you're welcome, M'Lord. er...forgiven?"

"Thank you, Dame Suze," I said, smiling. I had a feeling people didn't typically apologize to Suze. "Speaking of which, given that you just killed...well, trapped...a god and, oh yes, saved the world, I think we should probably find some better titles for you. And some income. And whatever the heck else you've ever dreamed of."

Herrún snickered quietly but stayed silent.

A flash of disgust went across Suze's face. "No thank you, M'Lord. As an Archmage I'll have no trouble making or acquiring whatever I need and, besides, money is a bit irrelevant now, isn't it? After what you did with the shops? As to titles and positions...." Her features had smoothed into the well-practiced smiling calm of a life-long servant. "...Why would I want to be a noble, M'Lord? I would prefer to simply make sure that my family and loved ones have the opportunity to go through the FLEA as well. Afterwards, we'll probably move out of the castle."

I bowed my head in acquiescence. "Whatever you like. Although, now that we aren't dealing with invading armies or world-ending crises, my next goal is to fix things in Flobovia. Get rid of indentured servitude, make it so that the nobles can't abuse their power the way they have been, fix...well, everything basically. If you wanted to, I could use an advisor on that." I smiled ruefully, rubbing my neck. "It hasn't escaped my attention that I tend to go off half-cocked, and that I often make a bigger mess than I solve. You are...really good at planning ahead, as this little escapade showed." I frowed. "Speaking of which, how exactly did you do all that?"

She hesitated, choosing her words carefully. "Your plan was...complicated, M'Lord." She saw the sting she'd delivered and hurried to smooth it over. "Oh, it made perfect sense for what we expected to face, and Strategos Albrecht simplified it and came up with all those backup plans but...nothing that was intended to cover what we actually faced."

She wasn't wrong. The original plan had been all kinds of fancy; fortunately, Albrecht had smoothed it down. His version had been fairly straightforward: Alpha Squad was to handle physical and magical defense; Thomas, Duncan, Unn, and Albrecht were to dispel any magic that would prevent us from escaping; Drifa and Colborn were 'free safeties', responsible for countermage shoots and any miscellaneous issues; Reynard and Isaac were to use stacked Celerities to get an action ahead of Neklos and create the singularity that would trap him. Those two hadn't known what they were going to be casting, of course, or even that it would be directed against Neklos. I had only revealed that part right before we left, on the paper I'd passed around.

Still...all of those roles had been deemed vital. How in the world had Suze pulled it off all on her own?

"I mean, I get the basics," I said. "You cast the Plane Shift to get us out of there, then interrupted it with a Celerity to get a readied action so you could create the singularity zero time before we vanished. And you were able to throw the antimatter because it's a free action to pull things from your spell component pouch—which, let me add, is still bullshit—and your spell component pouch just randomly contains any spell component you could need for any spell and blah blah Major Creation needs a sample of the material so antimatter and blah blah. What I want to know is how you knew it would work? Afi should have had a Dimensional Lock on his fortress that would prevent the Plane Shift from working. And Neklos should have been able to Celerity after you to go first and interrupt you. How the hell did that all go off?"

She shrugged. "Well, M'Lord...Afi wanted us to win so of course he would have taken down his Dimensional Lock. And if you're caught in a surprise round you don't get to act...even if you're a god." That hint of resentment was back on her face as she said, "And, of course, wouldn't you be surprised if a meek little servant, obviously terrified out of her mind, suddenly started casting spells at you?"

I laughed ruefully. "Yeah, I guess I would. So, what was the antimatter for? The whole point of the exercise was to not kill Neklos, just to trap him in the time dilation from the black hole. And besides, you specified 'enough to destroy a twenty foot radius' which wasn't enough to reach Nekl—oh." I shook my head in disgust at my own obtuseness. "Of course. The gems. You destroyed the gems so that Thomas and the others' spirits would be free and we could resurrect them after we escaped. Damn, that's slick." I nodded slowly, working back through the logic. "And you specified antimatter osmium, which means you probably heard about it from Isaac bitching about how I tricked him into blowing himself up...but how did you know different sizes would...? Ah, of course. You must have heard Albrecht talking to someone about how I said that I could have used the same technique to make bigger explosions and you figured it could scale down, too. That's why you specified 'enough to destroy a twenty foot radius' instead of 'twenty nanograms' or whatever it would be." I nodded again, impressed. "Slick. Very slick."

The curtsey she dropped me was only very slightly ironic, and she looked genuinely pleased—at least, if the blush and the smile still meant anything. My meek and mousey little Suze had just revealed an inner tigress that was requiring some adjustments. Still, that smile could still light up a room, and it was good to see.

"Ok, one last thing that I can't figure out," I said. "How is it that the Icefis didn't see all this in your head when they Mindscraped us in the Receiving Room? Or did they? I mean, I know that we figured they would 'Scrape us and see that we only intended to help Afi, but you were keeping secrets from the rest of us. How did you know they wouldn't spill them?"

She shrugged, a secretive smile tugging at the corners of her lips. "I used Programmed Amnesia on myself to hide all of those memories. Their Disjunction spells negated the Amnesia and I got the memories back, but that was after they had Mindscraped us. If they hadn't, I would have gotten them back as soon as we saw Death."

I grinned. "It's ok, you can say Neklos now. He's not going to be doing anything for a long, loooong time. Neklos! Neklos! Neklos! Neklos is a poopyhead! See?"

"Ahem," said the voice of the God of War who was suddenly standing right behind me. "That's my brother you're talking about."

So, here's the thing...standing in the middle of a ring of forty or fifty gods, all of whom are in full regalia, thirty feet tall, and lsooking pissed? Yeah, kinda scary.
