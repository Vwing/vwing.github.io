Almost everything was left untouched until it came to the poorly-installed fire door in the furthest, darkest corner of my mind.

The door was heavy metal, covered in gangland graffiti and plastered with 'Keep Out! This Means You!' signs. It was locked with multiple bars and a thick metal chain secured by a padlock the size of a toaster. None of that mattered; the maggot-presence effortlessly pushed those locks aside, swung the door open, and stepped inside.

The mountain exploded, killing Isaac just slightly too quickly to let him curse my name for tricking him to his death.

I suppressed a gleeful cackle as filth-dripping undead fangs tore Landguard after Landguard apart as they spent their lives like water to keep me, the author of their doom, safe and unharmed.

A comfortably middle-aged mother sat on a bench outside a house, smiling happily as she watched her children play tag in the backyard. Suddenly time slowed to a crawl; I was able to watch in great detail as a wall of light and fury and flame rolled over them all. I got to see them burn away over the course of long subjective seconds as the explosion I had used to kill Reynard and destroy the temple of Herrún rolled through the village of Yellow Pines.

All of those memories (real and imagined) and others were kept inside that dark room, only occasionally slipping out through the barriers I had set up to defend myself. The presence consideringly wriggled through all of it, and then wiped it away with the ease of a sponge wiping up a minor spill.

Suddenly I was back at the table with everyone else. I was shaking in fear, the feeling of those maggoty fingers still fresh in my brain. I groped for my Scotch, took a shaky sip—
