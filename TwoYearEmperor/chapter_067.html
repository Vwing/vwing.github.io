Rules Note: There's a bit of weirdness with how Grǫfhamr and the Weirdstone interact: in general, magical effects don't cross planar boundaries and, as has been remarked, each of the Portable Holes that make up Grǫfhamr is technically a separate plane. If the Weirdstone is outside the city then technically it would cover the area around the city, but its effect wouldn't reach into the city itself, meaning that a Scry Location could in fact look into the city. On the other hand, if the Weirdstone were inside the city, it wouldn't affect more than one Portable Hole so the same weakness to Scry Location would apply.

Portable Holes are a bit unusual, though. Other interplanar spells (e.g. Mage's Magnificent Mansion and Gate) create magical portals that leads to another plane. Portable Holes are simply...holes. Actual, physical holes. After thinking about it for a while, I decided that area effects would cross the boundary but non-AoE effects would not. So, for example, if you are standing just outside Grǫfhamr and you drop a Fireball right in front of the door, the fire will spread into the city. On the other hand, if you try to make the Fireball appear inside the city while you are outside, then the spell will fail. Ditto for trying to cast a Magic Missile through the door.

To balance this out, I decided that large scale AoE effect (especially the Weirdstone) would not emanate from inside Grǫfhamr normally. The inside of the city is physically connected to the outside, but its insides do not have a well-defined location in reference to the outside. So, if the Weirdstone were inside the city then it would shield the entire city and a line emanating straight out from the door. If it's outside the city, then it will cover its normal six mile radius, including the city.

In a nutshell: since the Weirdstone is outside the city its effect covers the normal area and extends into the city even though that technically requires crossing planar boundaries.
