Thomas's eyebrows shot up and his face split in a wide, friendly smile. "Oh! You're Afi! A real pleasure. I've been hoping to meet you for a long time; there's something I wanted to ask you."

Afi cocked his head in friendly curiosity. "What's that?"

"Well, I only know a little bit about liches," Thomas began. "Can I say lich, by the way? Is there a better term?" He sounded concerned, clearly not wanting to be offensive.

Afi waved in relaxed dismissal. "No, lich is fine. No worries."

Thomas looked relieved. "Oh, good. Anyway, I meant to ask—as a lich, you're a massively powerful wizard...er, sorceror?"

Afi nodded. "Wizard, yes. Well, and sorceror. And cleric. And a few other things. I try to pick up a new class every thousand years or so, just to keep things fresh. Finding the synergies between classes is fascinating."

"Yes, I can imagine," Thomas said, nodding in comprehension.

"Thomas, don't listen—" I began, terrified.

He spared me a brief glance and waved me quiet. "Don't worry, Jake. Clearly we've been misunderstanding Afi all this time. He seems like a great guy, and he said he just wants to have peace and work with us."

I shut up. This was pretty much my worst nightmare come to life; Thomas and the others had all been replaced by pod people, and any minute now Afi was going to decide that he didn't need me around and would make my head explode, or an alien burst out of my chest, or something equally fatal and disgusting. He was between me and the door, too...maybe I could grab a couple of the Landguard swords and shut off magic so as to isolate myself into a single Portable Hole? No, that wouldn't work; I'd just have to choose between suffocating or having to shut off the antimagic and being right back where I'd started. And that was assuming that they would even work for me; they might be locked to their bearers.

My mind was racing, plans flitting past, being reviewed and rejected at lightning speed. There had to be some way to survive this.

While I was frantically searching for options, Thomas was speaking again. "Anyway, I meant to ask: when you're in an antimagic field like the one Duncan just put up—sorry about that, by the way—isn't it true that you're just a squishy half-monkey? And I—"

His blades came into his hands so fast that they seemed to teleport. He kicked the table, sending it flying out of the way and knocking people over like tenpins; the instant it was out of his way, he blazed forward at inhuman speed, tapping his swords together as he went. He and Afi vanished from sight, taking the doorway with them.

Less than a minute later, he reappeared. He was soaked in blood, as were the walls, floor, and ceiling. Afi's dead body—now reverted to a skeleton—was lying at his feet in multiple pieces. The skull was impaled on Thomas's right-hand sword.

"...I am still a twentieth level paladin of the Land," Thomas concluded grimly.

We all just stared in shock.
